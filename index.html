A Struggle to Belong

A Memoir of Family, Race, and Resilience

About the Book

A deeply personal memoir tracing a young girl's journey through family, race, identity, and the long arc of resilience. This story moves between intimate memory and the broader forces that impacted her life, offering a powerful reflection on belonging.

About the Author

Journalist Carolyn K. Erwin has spent her career writing about Black life and culture. She began as a staff reporter for the Baltimore Afro‑American and later became an Assistant Editor for JET and EBONY magazines, where she told the stories of Black entertainers, public figures, educators, and athletes.

In her debut memoir, A Struggle to Belong, set in Baltimore, Carolyn turns the lens inward, tracing her childhood through the cultural shifts from segregation to Civil Rights. Her story is a universal story about the search for belonging, identity, and place in a world that keeps changing.
